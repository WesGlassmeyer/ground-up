LISBON'S
HIDDEN GEM
ON THE MAT

João Ferreira grew up in Porto and began training BJJ at 16 under Prof. Nuno Oliveira, one of the first generation of Portuguese black belts. After earning his own black belt in 2012, he relocated to Lisbon and founded Alfama BJJ in the city's oldest neighborhood.

A two-time Portuguese National Champion, Prof. Ferreira has competed across Europe including the European Championships in Lisbon and the British Open in London. He has produced multiple national-level competitors and dozens of international practitioners who have passed through his doors.

His classes are technical and methodical — reflecting his background as a secondary school science teacher before BJJ became his full-time career. All classes are taught in English and Portuguese.

VISITING
LISBON?

Lisbon has become one of the most popular cities in Europe for long-term travelers, remote workers, and tourists from the UK, US, and beyond. English is spoken everywhere — and that includes our mat.

We're based in Alfama, a 10-minute walk from the city center, and we see visiting practitioners every single week. The door is always open.
